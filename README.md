# Stop the ICE Facility in Surprise, AZ

Community-maintained resource tracking a proposed ICE detention facility in Surprise, Arizona, and organizing local opposition.

## 🚨 The Situation

On January 23, 2026, DHS purchased a 418,400 square foot warehouse at 13290 W Sweetwater Avenue for $70 million. The City of Surprise was not consulted before the purchase. The facility was originally planned for up to 1,500 detention beds; DHS has since told the city it will cap occupied beds at 542. The site sits on the same street as Legacy Traditional School (1,868 students).

On April 22, 2026, DHS issued a stop work order to the contractor, GardaWorld Federal Services. Two days later, Arizona Attorney General Kris Mayes filed suit against DHS and ICE in federal court, alleging violations of the National Environmental Policy Act and the Immigration and Nationality Act. The stop work order was rescinded on May 6, and site work resumed. On June 30, 2026, DHS and the state jointly asked the court to pause that litigation while DHS completes a required environmental assessment. Under the agreement, DHS has committed not to convert the warehouse or detain anyone at the facility until the assessment concludes. The order has no legal effect until a judge signs it.

On June 16, 2026, community organizers filed a petition with the Maricopa County Board of Supervisors seeking to disincorporate the City of Surprise, citing the council's refusal to formally oppose the facility.
