# Project Briefing: Stop ICE Detention Facility in Surprise, AZ

## Quick Context
Jason is a Surprise, Arizona resident organizing community opposition to a proposed 1,500-bed ICE detention facility. This is an active, time-sensitive campaign requiring ongoing updates, research, and material creation.

---

## The Situation

### Facility Details
- **Location:** 13290 W Sweetwater Avenue, Surprise, AZ 85379
- **Building:** 418,400 sq ft warehouse on 24 acres (formerly Rockefeller Group's Surprise Pointe Commerce Center)
- **Purchase:** $70 million cash by DHS, January 23, 2026
- **Planned capacity:** 1,500 beds
- **Critical fact:** City of Surprise was NOT consulted before purchase

### Nearby Sensitive Locations
- Legacy Traditional School (K-8, 1,868 students) — SAME STREET
- Marley Park community — 5,000+ residents, young families
- Copper Canyon Ranch — 2,830 residents, gated community
- Heritage Club community center

### Key Dates
- **Feb 4, 2026:** City Council meeting (1st opportunity to speak)
- **Feb 18, 2026:** Next council meeting
- Council meets 1st and 3rd Tuesdays at 6 PM

## Key Evidence Points (All Sourced)

### Minneapolis "Operation Metro Surge" (January 2026)
- $2-3 million police overtime in 12 days
- 3,000+ overtime hours in first 4 days
- 50-80% revenue decrease for businesses
- Schools in lockdown
- State filed federal lawsuit
- Sources: City of Minneapolis, CBS Minnesota, MN Attorney General

### Oklahoma City (January 2026) — SUCCESS
- Proposed 1,500-bed facility CANCELLED
- Property owners withdrew after community pressure
- Hours of testimony at city council
- Sources: KOCO, KGOU (NPR), News9

### Hanover County, VA (January 2026)
- Board of Supervisors formally opposed facility
- $1M+ annual tax revenue loss projected
- Property owner (Jim Pattison) withdrew from sale
- Sources: WVTF (NPR), NBC12, CBS6 Richmond

### Eloy Detention Center, AZ (Existing)
- "Deadliest immigration detention center in U.S." — Rep. Grijalva
- 16+ deaths including 5 suicides
- 9% of ALL detention deaths nationwide (1 of 250+ facilities)
- 53 complaints in 2 years
- Sources: Detention Watch Network, NPR, Phoenix New Times

2. Building was designed for industrial use, not human detention
3. City controls utilities (water, sewer, electrical)
4. Change of use requirements
5. Arizona could pass detention facility moratorium (like Illinois)
