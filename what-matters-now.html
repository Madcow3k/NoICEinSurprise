What Happened April 21

Next Meeting: Tuesday, May 5, 2026 • 6:00 PM

Surprise City Council regular meeting.

16000 N. Civic Center Plaza, Surprise. Council Chambers.

On April 21, the council again took no formal vote on the ICE facility. Councilmember Judd proposed putting a letter on the next agenda requesting DHS honor local zoning in the LD65 zone and Luke AFB restrictions. When Judd attempted to discuss the contents of the city's legal review, the city attorney intervened publicly, cautioning him against disclosing privileged information. Judd called for an executive session. Public comment was again overwhelmingly opposed.

DHS confirmed that the September opening target is “aggressive” and that a more realistic timeframe is “sometime in the fall.” Steel security gates are now visible around the warehouse. On April 15, Mayor Sartor told KTAR he would not pursue legal action. The mayor holds one of seven votes.

Five Findings

Each of the findings below is documented in a council record, a federal agency statement, a news outlet, or a public filing. Each links to the fuller account on the main site.

1
No Vote After Two Meetings

On April 7, Judd committed on the record to bringing the facility as a formal action item. On April 21, he proposed a letter instead. No resolution has been introduced. No vote has been taken. Other cities have passed formal resolutions, withheld water hookups, and filed lawsuits. Surprise has done none of these.

Read the April 21 and April 7 records →
2
Senators Demand a Halt by May 1

On April 16, Senators Kelly and Gallego sent a formal letter to DHS Secretary Mullin demanding a halt to all detention expansion in Arizona until full transparency and community engagement are completed. DHS has not responded to their original February letter. The senators set a May 1 response deadline. They specifically named Dysart High School and Dysart Middle School students.

Read the April 16 letter →
3
Still Nothing in Writing

Nine weeks after the DC trip (March 15 to 19), none of DHS's verbal commitments are in writing. The 542-bed cap, the facility tour, the school and senior center protections, the community relations board, the $300,000 annual property-tax reimbursement. None of it is enforceable.

Read the full record →
4
The Water Ordinance

Surprise passed a high-water-use ordinance requiring council approval for any facility using more than 100,000 gallons per day. The detention facility could use nearly 270,000 gallons per day at full capacity. The city's highest current user consumes 80,000 gallons per day. The ordinance is a concrete point of local authority the council has not yet exercised.

Read the water analysis →
5
The Legal Review Exists. The Attorney Won't Let Judd Talk About It.

On April 21, the city attorney intervened publicly when Judd tried to discuss the contents of the city's legal review. He cautioned Judd on the record. Judd called for an executive session to find out what he could and could not say. Something is in that review. The question for May 5 is: what is it, and why can't the public know?
