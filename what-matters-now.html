The Meeting

Tuesday, April 21, 2026 • 6:00 PM

Surprise City Council regular meeting.

16000 N. Civic Center Plaza, Surprise. Council Chambers.

At the April 7 meeting, Councilmember Chris Judd said on the record: “I want an item on the agenda to take action on the proposed DHS facility, potentially giving staff direction and permission to protect our local authority, our water supply, and our residents.” That motion has not yet come to a vote. April 21 is the next time it could.

On April 15, Mayor Kevin Sartor told KTAR he would not pursue any legal action. The mayor holds one of seven votes on the council.

Five Findings

Each of the findings below is documented in a council record, a federal agency statement, a news outlet, or a public filing. Each links to the fuller account on the main site.

1
The Council Math

The mayor holds one of seven votes. Before April 15, four of the six other councilmembers (Patrick Duffy, Chris Judd, Earle Greenburg, Johnny Melton) had each publicly raised concerns about the facility. That is already a majority of the seven-member council without the mayor.

Read the April 15 and April 7 records →
2
The City Was Not Consulted

DHS Secretary Kristi Noem confirmed in a February 23, 2026 written response to Rep. Paul Gosar that ICE “did not have direct contact with the City of Surprise or Maricopa County before site selection.” The $70 million cash purchase on January 23 was the first the city learned of it. This is DHS’s own admission on the public record, not the city’s account.

Read the DHS confirmation →
3
Still Nothing in Writing

The council met with DHS in Washington on March 19. DHS made four verbal commitments: a capacity cap of 542 beds, a facility tour before opening, a pledge to stay out of schools and senior centers, and a community relations board. On April 14, seven weeks after the DC trip, the mayor confirmed the city is still “working on” putting these into a memorandum of understanding. None of the commitments are enforceable.

Read the April 14 and March 23 records →
4
The Water Ordinance

Surprise passed a high-water-use ordinance requiring council approval for any facility using more than 100,000 gallons per day. City Manager Andrea Davis estimated the detention facility could use nearly 270,000 gallons per day at full capacity. The city’s highest current water user consumes 80,000 gallons per day. The ordinance is a concrete point of local authority the council has not yet exercised.

Read the water analysis →
5
AROCC, Forty Miles Away

On April 9, Representatives Stanton, Ansari, and Grijalva made an unannounced oversight visit to the Arizona Removal Operations Coordination Center at Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport. AROCC was designed for 157 people and hold times under 12 hours. Arizona Mirror’s analysis of ICE data showed a 2026 average daily population of 274, an average hold of 36 hours, and one person held 18 days. The congressional delegation described concrete rooms without beds, blankets, or showers. This is the existing Arizona facility operating under the same federal standards DHS cites for Surprise.
